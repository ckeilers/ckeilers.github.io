Creators: Chloe Keilers, Raymond Yao.
Blender, OpenGL, Raytracing
December 2019
This is our final project for CS 148, an introduction to computer graphics. We wanted to create an image of fantasy and including a focus on a reflexive surface. Inspired by the upcoming winter break and a beautiful picture of an aurora, we decided on a little snowman under a metallic lamp that reflected the room as the reference image shows.
Each of us created several objects in the room include the snowman with the hat, the lamp, and the computer monitor. Our first draft was in OpenGL with horrible lighting.
While we continued to find objects and texture images for the rest of the room, we used Blender to arrange objects, place lighting, and test camera angles. In lieu of a flat background in the first draft, we put holiday movie posters on the background wall - Rudolph Poster, Frosty Poster, and the Grinch Poster. The computer monitor is a screenshot of the class’s forum page with a cheeky request from the snowman on how to get an A.
We ran into some trouble when translating our final vision from Blender to raytracing, specifically with a semi-transparent cone with an aurora texture. The cone could either be transparent, but only let the ray bounce once or reflexive.
One test we did try was changing the color of the lights to see what was going through which resulted in a cool picture but alas no solution.
Due to time constraints we had to remove the cone for the final image. The final raytrace rendering took 24 hours to compile.

Creator: Chloe Keilers
Blender
October 2019
While browsing NASA's many galleries and archives of their various rover, telescope, and other space-related missions, I came across Voyager in NASA’s 3D gallery. On a separate adventure, I had studied Roman and Italian architecture. Following Vitruvius's guideline, the only Roman architect who wrote a book, I made a simple Roman temple object in Blender. What would happened if we combined both NASA’s space gallery and old ancient buildings?
My first image threw objects together without much care - but it started the idea of creating an anachronistic landscape in front of a colorful galaxy.
Instead of a flat, sculpted plain for the lunar landscape, I explored NASA’s 3D galaxy some more for something more detailed like Gale Crater and also added another self made building based on Il Duomo. I finally added both Voyager and one of the famous Martian twin rover.
The landscape needed a bit more texture and the Earth needed a storm to round out the final image.
